Akara Recipe

Akara is a popular Nigerian street food made from black-eyed peas, also known as cowpeas. It's a deep-fried bean cake that's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Here's a simple recipe for making Nigerian Akara:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups black-eyed peas
  • 1-2 scallions or onions
  • 1-2 chili peppers (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Water (for blending)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Black-Eyed Peas:
    • Rinse the black-eyed peas thoroughly in cold water.
    • Soak the beans in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight to soften them.
  2. Peel the Beans:
    • After soaking, peel off the skin from the beans. This can be done easily by rubbing the beans between your palms or using a blender to blend and then rinsing in water to remove the skin.
  3. Blend the Beans:
    • If using a blender, blend the peeled beans with the scallions or onions and chili peppers until you have a smooth paste. Add a little water if necessary to aid the blending process.

Akara is often enjoyed as a breakfast or snack item and is typically served with spicy sauce, pap, or bread. Adjust the spice level to your preference by adding more or fewer chili peppers. Enjoy your homemade Nigerian Akara!
